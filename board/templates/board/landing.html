Geospatial Technology

By UPSC Editorial Team | Last Updated: January 5, 2026 | Reading Time: 12 min
Overview: Geospatial technology collects, processes, and illustrates Earth's spatial data. This comprehensive guide covers types, applications, government initiatives, and challenges of Geospatial Technology in India.

Understanding Geospatial Technology

Geospatial Technology refers to equipment, software, and methods used to acquire, process, and analyse data with a geographic component associated with specific Earth locations.

Applications of Geospatial Technology

  • Climate Change and Disaster Management: Improves situational awareness for natural disasters
  • Healthcare: Tracks virus spread and identifies high-risk areas
  • Agriculture: Assesses vegetation health in specific areas
  • Transport: Location tracking and route navigation

National Geospatial Policy, 2022

The Government of India introduced a National Geospatial policy to develop a coherent framework and enable easy availability of valuable data to businesses and public.

Important for UPSC: The National Geospatial Policy 2022 aims to establish the Geospatial Data Promotion and Development Committee (GDPDC) as the premier body for strategizing developments. [Reference: DST Guidelines 2021]

Government Initiatives

NAVIC

The Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System consists of 7 satellites for terrestrial, aerial and marine navigation, disaster management, and vehicle tracking.

Practice Questions

  2. How can geospatial technology aid in disaster management? (150 words)

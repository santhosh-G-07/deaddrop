Geospatial Technology

By UPSC Editorial Team | Last Updated: January 2, 2026 | Reading Time: 12 min
Overview: Geospatial technology collects, processes, and illustrates Earth's spatial data. This comprehensive guide covers types, applications, government initiatives, and challenges of Geospatial Technology in India - essential for UPSC Mains GS Paper 3.

Understanding Geospatial Technology

Geospatial Technology refers to the equipment, software, and methods used to acquire, process, and analyse data that has a geographic or spatial component. This means that the data is associated with a specific location on the Earth's surface.

It is fundamental to fields such as cartography, remote sensing, and Geographic Information Systems (GIS). Geospatial technology offers a critical understanding of spatial configurations, interconnections, and events.

Applications of Geospatial Technology

The range of applications for geospatial data is extensive. Some standard instances include:

  • Climate Change and Disaster Management: Improves situational awareness and decision-making for natural disasters
  • Earth Observation: Monitors plant growth, water quality, temperatures, ocean salt levels
  • Healthcare: Tracks virus spread, identifies high-risk areas, supports migrant populations
  • Land and Forest Management: Village surveys, mapping, accurate rural land records
  • Agriculture: Assesses vegetation health in specific areas
  • Transport: Location tracking, arrival time prediction, route navigation

Geospatial Technology in India

With its wide geographical expanse, Geospatial technology is key to India's development:

Key Statistics: As per the India Geospatial Artha Report 2021, the geospatial market in India stands at Rs 38,972 crore, employing around 4.7 lakh individuals. The sector is projected to expand to Rs 63,100 crore by 2025.

Institutions like the Survey of India (SoI), ISRO, Remote Sensing Application Centers (RSACs), and the National Informatics Centre (NIC) have played pivotal roles in advancing geospatial technology.

National Geospatial Policy, 2022

The Government of India introduced a National Geospatial policy with two main goals:

  1. Develop a coherent national framework
  2. Enable easy availability of valuable Geospatial data to businesses and public

Key Milestones:

  • By 2025: Implement supportive policy and legal structure
  • By 2030: Achieve high-resolution topographical surveys and mapping
  • By 2035: Obtain precise Bathymetric Geospatial Data and create National Digital Twin

Government Initiatives

NAVIC (Navigation with Indian Constellation)

The Indian Regional Navigation Satellite System (IRNSS) consists of 7 satellites and ground stations. Applications include:

  • Terrestrial, aerial and marine navigation
  • Disaster management and vehicle tracking
  • Integration with mobile phones
  • Precise timing for ATMs and power grids

BHUVAN Portal

A web portal designed to locate and access geographic data and related services. Key features:

  • Reflects accurate country borders based on Government of India data
  • Better privacy protection than foreign map applications
  • Supports Atmanirbhar Bharat mission
Important for UPSC: The National Geospatial Policy 2022 aims to establish the Geospatial Data Promotion and Development Committee (GDPDC) as the premier body for strategizing geospatial sector developments. [Reference: DST Guidelines 2021]

Challenges of Geospatial Technology

  • Underdeveloped market: Limited awareness among stakeholders
  • Shortage of skilled personnel: Lack of trained professionals
  • Data access issues: Absence of high-resolution foundational data
  • Customized solutions: India lacks solutions for unique challenges

Way Forward

Several measures can promote Geospatial technology in India:

  1. Geospatial Portal and Cloud: Develop portal for 'data as a service'
  2. Collaborative culture: Foster data sharing and joint efforts
  3. Academic programs: Launch undergraduate courses at IITs and NITs
  4. Foundation data development: Indian National Digital Elevation Model (InDEM)
Conclusion: Geospatial technology is crucial for India's development across multiple sectors including defense, agriculture, disaster management, and urban planning. Understanding its applications and challenges is essential for UPSC preparation.
